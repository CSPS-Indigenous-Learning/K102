Who are the Métis people?

Historically, Métis were known to be adventurous, and hardworking individuals. They worked as voyageurs, buffalo hunters, boatmen, fishermen, traders, small business owners, clerks, loggers, farmers and cattlemen.

Highly regarded as interpreters and translators, Métis served as intermediaries between First Nations peoples and European settlers. Well known for their skills, Métis people were often employed by fur-trading companies and the North West Mounted Police.
