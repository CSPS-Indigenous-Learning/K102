Question of

True of False?

The term Métis has evolved over time. Today, the term “Métis” is used in two ways:

  • It is a term individuals use to identify themselves as someone of mixed heritage
  • It is one of the “Aboriginal peoples of Canada” within the meaning of section 35(2) of the Constitution Act, 1982

 

Answer: True

As public service employees, we need to understand and respect an individual’s decision to identify as Métis, even if they may not possess Métis rights as defined by the Supreme Court under section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982.
