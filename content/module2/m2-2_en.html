Métis individuals

Let’s see an example of three individuals who identify as Métis.

  • Lucie
  • Elder Reta Gordon
  • Natalie

Lucie

Lucie was born in Rouyn-Noranda in Abitibi-Témiscamingue. She is Métis from Québec and was proudly raised in the traditional culture, following her mother footsteps:

  • Her mother hunted each year to feed her family and was known for being good at moose calling
  • She belonged to the Native Alliance of Quebec, which supports Indigenous people living off reserves

As a facilitator with the Indigenous Learning Series, Lucie is a proud Métis woman and continues to support the Alliance community.

Elder Reta Gordon

Elder Reta Gordon has always known her identity. She is from the Métis Nation and is a founding member of the Métis Nation of Ontario.

Proud of her Métis culture, Elder Reta Gordon is always open to sharing her knowledge. She understands how important it is to educate people about Métis culture and governance and explain what makes Métis distinct from the other Indigenous groups in Canada.

Natalie

Natalie was raised in Kapuskasing in Northern Ontario as a non-Indigenous person. She’s always been connected to the land and curious about culture. As a facilitator with the Indigenous Learning Series, she decided to investigate her own heritage. She discovered that:

  • on her father’s side, she could retrace her roots to the first officially recorded marriage in New France (Quebec) between a French man and an Indigenous woman (Huron Nation) in the Catholic Church
  • on her mother’s side, she was related to Jean Nicolet, a coureur des bois who married an Indigenous woman in a traditional ceremony during his stay in the Nipissing territory.

Natalie is still pursuing her genealogical inquiries. She’s on a journey of learning and reflection about privilege, identity, genetics, community and belonging.
