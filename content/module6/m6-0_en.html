Conclusion

In the last decades, the voice of Métis people has been heard. Although their identity is still a major factor of contention, Métis people remain strong and present. They will not be forgotten.

The following examples provide links to some interesting stories about Métis people.

  • Heather Kashman had an impressive hockey career as a player and coach at the provincial and national levels; in 2011, she played in the Canada Winter Games and was voted the most valuable female hockey player in Alberta
  • Pearl Calahasen was the first Métis woman elected to public office in Alberta and served as Associate Minister of Aboriginal Affairs
  • Senator Gerry St. Germain, the first self-identified Métis person to serve in Canada’s federal Cabinet, has dedicated much of his life’s work to advancing issues of importance to Indigenous peoples across Canada
  • Maria Campbell is a Métis author (Halfbreed, 1973), playwright, broadcaster, filmmaker and Elder, who speaks four languages fluently: Cree, Michif, Saulteaux and English
  • Suzanne Rochon-Burnett was a Métis business woman and a founder of the Métis Nation of Ontario
  • Gabriel Dumont was a prominent Métis leader and political figure, best known for his movements within the North-West Resistance at the Battles of Batoche, Fish Creek and Duck Lake.
