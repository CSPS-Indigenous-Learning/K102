Answer: All of the above

Louis Riel is a central figure in Indigenous Canadian history. He was a leader among the Métis people of the Red River. In 1869–1870, he headed a provisional government that eventually negotiated the Manitoba Act within the Government of Canada. The Act established Manitoba as a province and provided some protection for French-language rights.

Question of

True or False?

The Métis Scrip was a paper that entitled Métis to land or money. However, the real purpose of the Métis Scrip was for the Government to gain control over the land.

Answer: True

When Métis people received land allowances (or financial equivalents) from the Government of Canada, they were usually granted in the form of a paper scrip known as the Métis scrip. The Métis scrip policy specifically aimed to extinguish Métis title, much as the historic treaties did for First Nations.
