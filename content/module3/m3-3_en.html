Louis Riel, Métis Leader

Louis Riel emerged as a Métis leader. The Métis people of the Red River and many people in Quebec considered him a hero. When conflict arose between the Government of Canada and Louis Riel, he fled to the United States to avoid being killed. As a fugitive, he was elected three times to the House of Commons of Canada, although he was never able to take his seat in the house.

Métis people were initially the predominant force in the new province of Manitoba. However, delays in implementing the provisions of the Manitoba Act, 1870 and an increase in European settlers soon led to the Métis being marginalized. A second conflict broke out in 1885, which resulted in the defeat of Métis forces by federal troops and the execution of Louis Riel.
