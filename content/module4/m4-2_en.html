Question of

True or False?

The first treaty relationship with Métis dates back to the Manitoba Act, 1870. No further treaties have been established.

 

Answer: False

Agreements have been finalized with the Sahtu Dene and Métis, the Gwich’in and the Tlicho. Negotiations are currently underway with the Northwest Territory Métis Nation and Métis who are represented in the Dehcho Process.

Question of

True or False?

Métis are still negotiating with the federal and provincial governments to develop treaties and self-government agreements that will better position the Métis in Canada moving forward.

Answer: True

In December 2015, the Government of Canada began negotiating with Métis groups in response to the Manitoba Métis Federation Decision. The Government of Canada is currently engaged in the recognition and implementation of Indigenous rights.
