Chapter 3: Governance and treaties

Métis National Council

In 1983, the Métis National Council was created to achieve a clear definition and defence of Métis rights and to act as a united voice on behalf of Métis. The Council was formed by representatives from elected, province-based governance structures.

The Government of Canada and the Métis National Council and its Governing Members signed the Canada-Métis Nation Accord in April 2017.

The Accord:

  • marks a significant step towards a renewed nation-to-nation, government-to-government relationship based on recognition of rights, respect, co-operation and partnership
  • outlines the ways in which the Government of Canada and the Métis Nation will work together to set priorities and co-develop distinctions-based policy in areas of shared interest

The Métis National Council’s stated goal is “to secure a healthy space for the Métis Nation’s ongoing existence within the Canadian federation.” They have five current affiliates:

  • Métis Nation of Ontario
  • Manitoba Métis Federation
  • Métis Nation Saskatchewan
  • Métis Nation of Alberta
  • Métis Nation British Columbia

The Métis National Council also created the Women of the Métis Nation Association, which provides a platform for Métis women to promote and preserve their social, political and economic interests.

Did you know?

There are multiple self-representing Métis organizations on the rise in the Eastern regions in Canada.
