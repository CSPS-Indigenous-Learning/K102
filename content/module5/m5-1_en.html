Contemporary realities

“My people will sleep for 100 years, and when they awake, it will be the artists who give them back their spirit.”
Louis Riel

It has now been over 100 years since Louis Riel predicted the rise of the Métis Nation.

Following a long period of dispossession, injustice and impoverishment, the Métis Nation has re-emerged as a powerful, united and distinct population in Canada. It has thrived despite the racist and assimilationist government policies and practices of the past.

Throughout history, Métis people have acted collectively to protect their lands and fight for their rights. They have defended their existence as a distinct Indigenous people and nation within the Canadian federation.

This dedication continues today. Citizens and communities are cultivating their distinct culture, traditions, language and lifestyle while pursuing social and economic development.

Many Métis families today retain their traditional approach to life and support each other through extended family ties:

  • Food obtained from hunting and fishing parties is shared with Elders and others
  • Family members pass traditional knowledge on to their children
  • Traditional herbal medicine and a holistic family-patient approach are often combined with western medicine
